Transformers are electrical devices used to transfer electrical energy between two or more coils of wire through electromagnetic induction. They consist of two coils, known as the primary and secondary winding, typically wound around a shared iron core. When an alternating current (AC) flows through the primary winding, it creates a changing magnetic field in the iron core. This changing magnetic field induces a voltage in the secondary winding, allowing for the transformation of voltage levels. Transformers are essential in power distribution and transmission, as they can step up voltage for long-distance transmission or step down voltage for safe and efficient use in homes. They are highly efficient devices with a wide range of applications, including in power substations, electrical appliances, and electronic equipment.
